QParcel came to me with a brand that had outgrown itself. Founded in 2019 as a regional carrier, they'd expanded to five countries but still looked like a local startup.

The brief: make QParcel feel like a trusted European logistics player without losing the energy and directness that made them successful in the first place.

## The Approach

We started with positioning before touching any visual. What did QParcel actually stand for? Talking to their team and customers, two things kept coming up: speed and honesty. They ship fast and they tell you exactly what's happening with your parcel.

That became the brand premise: *Straight lines, delivered.*

## Visual Direction

The identity leans on bold geometric forms — a wordmark built from near-perfect rectangles, a custom icon mark that doubles as a route and a parcel — and a palette that's confident without being aggressive.

The yellow came from the original brand. We kept it, sharpened it, made it the single accent against a clean near-white and a deep graphite.
