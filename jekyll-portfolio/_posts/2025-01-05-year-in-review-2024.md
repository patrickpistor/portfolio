Every year I write one of these, and every year I'm surprised by how different the year was from what I expected when I started it.

## Projects Worth Mentioning

**QParcel** — a brand refresh and website for a parcel startup — was the project I learned the most from. The brief was clear and constrained, and I spent more time fighting the constraint than working with it. Eventually I stopped. The thing that clicked was accepting that good design within limits is more interesting than good design without them.

**Terrain Studio** was the opposite: almost no constraints. Full creative control. And somehow harder. When anything goes, you have to justify every decision from first principles. It's exhausting and clarifying.

## What Changed in How I Work

I started doing more written thinking before opening any design tool. A page of notes, badly formatted, thinking through the problem in words. It slows the start down and speeds the middle up.

I also stopped treating feedback as an obstacle. Clients push back because they care. The push-back is information. Most of the time, the thing they're reacting to is a signal that something isn't landing the way I intended — not that they're wrong about the direction.

## The Lesson I Keep Relearning

The work I'm most proud of rarely comes from the brief. It comes from a conversation around the brief, a constraint I added myself, or a problem I noticed that wasn't being asked about.

The brief tells you what the client thinks they need. Understanding what they actually need is the job.

Here's to 2025.
